Ein Überblick über die Technologie VR/AR

Definition von VR und AR nach Experten

  • “VR technology combines multiple human–computer interfaces to provide various sensations (visual, haptic, auditory, etc.), which give the user a sense of presence in the virtual world.”
    (Seth, A., Vance, J. und Oliver, J.) [1]
  • „Virtual Reality (VR) refers to the use of three-dimensional displays and interaction devices to explore real-time computer-generated environments.“
    (Steve Bryson) [2]
  • „Virtual Reality refers to immersive, interactive, multi-sensory, viewer-centered, three-dimensional computer-generated environments and the combination of technologies required to build these environments.“
    (Carolina Cruz-Neira) [3]
  • „The promise of immersive virtual environments is one of a three-dimensional environment in which a user can directly perceive and interact with three-dimensional virtual objects. The underlying belief motivating most virtual reality (VR) research is that this will lead to more natural and effective human-computer interfaces.“
    (Mine) [4]
